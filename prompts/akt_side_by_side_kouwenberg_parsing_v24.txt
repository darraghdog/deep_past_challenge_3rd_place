------------------------KOUWENBERG TO LARSEN ENGLISH STYLE GUIDE------------------------
This guide converts Kouwenberg's English translation style to Larsen/OAA standard.
The source translations are already in English but need style normalization.

## Vocabulary Normalization (Kouwenberg → Larsen)

### Commodities & Materials
- "cloth(s)" / "garment(s)" → "textile(s)"
- "lead" (when clearly annukum) → "tin"
- "money" → "silver" (when kaspum)

### People & Professions
- "servant" (business context) → "employee"
- "female slave" → "slave-girl"
- "lawyer" / "commissioner" → "attorney"
- "messenger" / "traveler" → "traveller"
- "packer" → "transporter"

### Financial & Legal Terms
- "money bag" / "purse" → keep as "naruqqum" (italicized concept)
- "trading station" → keep as "kārum"
- "outpost" / "way station" → keep as "wubārtum"
- "capital" → "working capital" (for be'ūlātum)
- "toll" / "duty" → "transport tariff"
- "toll station" → "road-tax"

### Letter Formulas
Normalize to Dataset_Instructions.txt patterns:
- "Say to X, thus Y:" → "To X: Thus Y:"
- "X says to Y:" → "To Y: Thus X:"

### Terms to Keep Untranslated
These Akkadian terms should remain in the translation:
- kārum (trading colony)
- wubārtum (way station)
- naruqqum (joint-stock fund)
- hamuštum (5-day week, when specific)

### Special Notes
- "City" (capitalized) always refers to Assur
- "city" (lowercase) refers to local Anatolian towns
- Preserve all bracket notation [...] for damaged text
- Keep proper name normalizations with diacritics (ā, ē, ī, ū, š, ṣ, ṭ)
