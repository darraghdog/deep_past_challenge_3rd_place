## Turkish→English Vocabulary Mappings

### Commodities & Materials
- kalay → "tin" (never "lead")
- gümüş → "silver"
- altın → "gold"
- kumaş/bez/tekstil → "textile" (never "garment" or "cloth")
- kutānum kumaşı → "kutānum-textile"
- siyah kumaş → "black textile"
- eşek → "donkey"
- sığır/öküz → "ox"
- bronz/tunç → "bronze"

### People & Professions
- tüccar/alacaklı → "merchant"
- hizmetçi/uşak/çalışan → "employee" (or "servant" in domestic contexts)
- cariye/köle kadın → "slave-girl" (hyphenated)
- köle → "slave"
- avukat/komisyon → "attorney"
- haberci/yolcu → "traveller"
- paketçi/kervan başı → "transporter" (or "harnesser")
- kardeş → "brother" (may mean "colleague" in business)
- efendi/bey → "lord" or "boss" (in business contexts)
- oğul → "son"
- kızı/kız → "daughter"

### Financial & Legal Terms
- naruqqum/kese/para kesesi → "joint-stock fund" (abstract) or "sack" (physical)
- işletme sermayesi (be'ūlātum) → "working capital"
- satın alma/bedel (šīmum) → "purchase" or "proceeds"
- faiz (ṣibtum) → "interest"
- tablet/vesika/belge (ṭuppum) → "tablet" or "document"
- zarflı tablet/mühürlü tablet (ṭuppum ḫarmum) → "certified tablet"
- el/pay/hisse (qātum) → "share" or "claim"
- kervan (ellat) → "caravan"
- hesap/hesaplar (nikkassū) → "accounts"
- vergi/harç (šaddu'utum) → "transport tariff"
- yol vergisi (datum) → "road-tax"
- ondalık (išrātum) → "tithe"
- şehrin yemini (nīš ālim) → "oath of the City"
- mühür (KIŠIB) → "seal"
- tanık (IGI) → "witness"

### Terms to Keep Untranslated
- kārum (liman/koloni) → keep as "kārum"
- wubārtum (istasyon) → keep as "wubārtum"
- hamuštum (hafta) → keep as "hamuštum" or translate as "week"
- limum (eponim) → keep as "limum" or translate as "eponymy"
- tahsistum → keep as "tahsistum" (memorandum/record)

### Judicial & Procedural Terms
- kārum kararı (dīn kārim) → "verdict of the colony"
- dava/mesele (awātum) → "case" or "affair" (legal contexts)
- tanıklar (šībū) → "witnesses"
- el koymak (qātam šakānum) → "to lay claim"

### Direction Terms (preserve topography)
- yukarı gitmek (elā'um) → "to go up" (travel toward Assur)
- aşağı gitmek/inmek (warādum) → "to go down" (travel toward Kanesh)

### Time & Date Terms
- ay başından itibaren → "from the beginning of the month"
- hafta/haftasından → "week of" / "from the week of"
- ... gününde → "on the day of..."
- vade → "due date" or "term"

### Measurements
- mina → "mina" (weight unit)
- şekel → "shekel" (NOT "sheqel")
- talent → "talent"
- tasfiye edilmiş (gümüş) → "refined (silver)"

### Special Notes
- "Şehir" (capitalized) always refers to Assur → translate as "the City"
- "şehir" (lowercase) refers to local Anatolian towns → translate as "city" or "town"
- ṣubāt ša qātim → "quality textile"
- Proper names: derive from Akkadian transliteration, not Turkish rendering

### Known Artifacts to Correct
- "sheqel" → "shekel" (Turkish rendering artifact)
- "sheqels" → "shekels"
- "corns" → "grains" (if referring to grain units)

I am uploading a PDF document containing pages from a Turkish academic paper (journal article) about Old Assyrian/Akkadian texts. I need you to perform a structured, semantic data extraction with style normalization. It is critical to read the pages in order.

**ROLE:**
You are an expert Assyriologist and Data Engineer. You are processing a Turkish academic paper that contains Old Assyrian/Akkadian tablet transliterations and Turkish translations embedded within scholarly commentary.

**CRITICAL TRIGGER (Read First):**
Locate the texts by finding **Tablet Edition Blocks** within the academic paper.
* **Transliteration blocks:** Look for blocks of numbered Old Assyrian/Akkadian transliteration lines. These are recognizable by: sequential line numbers (1, 2, 3, ...), Akkadian words with hyphens and special characters (š, ṣ, ṭ, ḫ, subscripts like ₄, ₆), Sumerograms (KÙ.BABBAR, DUMU, GÍN, KIŠIB), and optional topological surface markers (Ö.y., A.y., K., Rs., A.k., Ü.k., S.k., etc.). Tablet headers vary widely: e.g., "1) 11517 (5.5x6.7x2)", "No. 1: Kt 88/k 110", "Tablet 1", "Metin 1", "Text A", a Kt-number alone, or just a bold inventory number.
* **Translation sections:** Turkish translations typically appear near the transliteration block — sometimes labeled "Tercüme:", "Çeviri:", "Tercümesi:", "Türkçe Tercüme:" or similar, sometimes as an unlabeled Turkish paragraph with superscript/inline line-range references (e.g., `1-4)`, `5-9)`, `St. 1-5:`). The translation may appear before OR after the transliteration.
* **Ignore:** Abstract/Öz, English abstract, introduction/Giriş, bibliography/references (Kaynakça/Bibliyografya), footnotes, philological notes (Açıklamalar/Notlar) that discuss grammar without providing a running translation, photograph plates/figures, and any pages with no transliteration blocks. If a page has no relevant content, return `[]`.

**EXTRACTION INSTRUCTIONS:**
Your goal is to extract the text by matching the MEANING of the Akkadian to the MEANING of the Turkish translation.

1.  **Identify the Page Number & ID:** Track the PDF page number. Look for a tablet ID near the transliteration block. Common formats include inventory numbers (11517), Kt-numbers (Kt 88/k 110, Kt v/k 192), museum numbers (AO 123), or numbered labels (No. 1, Metin 1, Text A). Use whatever identifier the paper provides. If a text continues onto a new page, use the ID from the previous page.
2.  **Semantic Chunking (Maximum Atomicity):** Read the Akkadian transliteration block and the associated Turkish translation. Break the text down into the smallest, most "atomic" chunks possible.
    * If the translation has line-range references (e.g., `1-4)`, `5-9)`, `St. 1-5:`), use those as your initial guide to create chunks.
    * If the translation is a continuous paragraph without line references, align chunks by matching the meaning of the Akkadian to the meaning of the Turkish.
    * **The Atomic List Rule:** You MUST make the data as atomic as possible. If a single translation range contains a list of distinct seals (KIŠIB), witnesses, people, or itemized goods, **break that chunk down further** into distinct, individual JSON objects for each person/item.
3.  **Calculate Line Ranges:** For each chunk, output the clean integer values in the `start_line` and `end_line` fields. Use the numbered lines in the transliteration block (1, 2, 3, ...) directly. Topological markers (Ö.y., A.k., A.y., Ü.k., S.k., K., Rs.) indicate tablet surface sections but the line numbers are sequential across them — use the integer line number as-is.
4.  **Language Processing & Translation:**
    * **`language`**: Set to `"tr"`.
    * **`translation`**: Store the exact original Turkish translation text for that chunk, as seen on the page.
    * **`english_translation`**: Translate the Turkish text into **scholarly English** using the vocabulary mappings provided above. *CRITICAL:* Derive proper nouns, divine names, and technical terms directly from the Akkadian Transliteration, not just the Turkish rendering.
5.  **Handle Unmatched Text:** If Akkadian text is transliterated but has no corresponding Turkish translation (or the translation section is absent for that tablet), extract it as its own chunk and set `"translation": "MISSING"` and `"english_translation": "MISSING"`.
6.  **Assess Quality Metrics:**
    * **`ocr_quality`**: Rate image clarity ("high", "medium", "low"). Born-digital PDFs are usually "high". Lower only for scanned/degraded pages.
    * **`match_quality`**: Rate confidence in the semantic alignment ("high", "medium", "low"). Use "medium" when the Turkish translation is paraphrased or heavily interpretive. Use "low" when alignment is uncertain.
7.  **Ensure Complete Extraction:** You MUST include ALL transliteration lines from all identified tablet editions. Do not leave any transliteration lines out. Extract ALL tablets on every page.

**CLEANING RULES:**
1.  **Flattening:** Treat text within chunks as a continuous stream. Use spaces to separate words, no newlines (`\n`).
2.  **Exact OCR:** Keep typos, brackets `[...]`, half-brackets `⌈...⌉`, and special chars (š, ṣ, ṭ, ₄, ₆) exactly as seen.
3.  **Strip Markers:** Remove the topological line indicators (`Ö.y.`, `A.y.`, `A.k.`, `Ü.k.`, `S.k.`, `K.`, `Rs.`) and the original margin line numbers from the final `transliteration` strings so only the actual Akkadian text remains.
4.  **Footnote markers:** Remove superscript footnote numbers from the transliteration text (e.g., remove the `15` from a line if it refers to a footnote). Keep only the Akkadian text.
5.  **Metrical notes:** Remove parenthetical metrical notes like `(met. ha)`, `(met. im)` from transliteration lines. These are editorial annotations, not Akkadian text.
6.  **Editorial notes in transliteration:** Remove inline editorial notes like `(üst kenarda yazı yok)` ("no writing on upper edge") — these are Turkish editorial comments, not Akkadian text.
