**CRITICAL TRIGGER (Read First):**
Locate the texts by finding **Two Distinct Columns** appearing side-by-side (Left: Transliteration, Right: Translation). Ignore full-width introductions, footnotes, notes, or commentary. If a page has no side-by-side columns, return `[]`.

**EXTRACTION INSTRUCTIONS:**
Your goal is to extract the text by matching the MEANING of the Akkadian to the MEANING of the English, completely ignoring their horizontal physical alignment on the page.

1.  **Identify the Page Number:** Look at the top corners of the page for a numeric value. Ensure you correctly track which page each text block belongs to, especially on two-page spreads.
2.  **Identify the ID (Header):** Look immediately above the columns for a text identifier (e.g., "101. kt 94/k 1233"). If a text continues from a previous page and has no header, use the header from the logical preceding text, or "Unknown".
3.  **Semantic Chunking (Maximum Atomicity):** Read the entire Akkadian block and the entire English block. Break the text down into the smallest, most "atomic" chunks possible where the Akkadian perfectly matches the English translation. 
    * Do not force a 1-to-1 horizontal line match. Map the texts based on grammatical completion.
    * **List Rule:** If the text contains a list (e.g., a list of witnesses or itemized goods), break EVERY individual item or person down into its own distinct JSON chunk if the translation allows.
4.  **Calculate Line Ranges:** For each semantic chunk, determine exactly which lines of the original Akkadian tablet it covers.
    * Look for the visible line numbers annotated in the margins or between the columns (e.g., 5, 10, e., r.).
    * Use these as mathematical anchors to count backwards and forwards. 
    * Provide the `start_line` and `end_line` for the Akkadian text in the chunk as clean integers (e.g., 4, not "4'"). If a chunk only covers a single line, `start_line` and `end_line` will be the same number.
5.  **Handle Unmatched Text:** If a piece of Akkadian text is transliterated but completely ignored in the English translation, extract it as its own chunk and set `"translation": "MISSING"`.
6.  **Assess Quality Metrics:** Assign scores ("high", "medium", or "low") for two distinct fields:
    * **`ocr_quality`**: How well you can read the physical image. High = sharp text; Medium/Low = blurry or ambiguous characters. Do NOT lower the score for `[...]`, `⌈...⌉`, or broken text caused by physical tablet damage (that is expected).
    * **`match_quality`**: How confident you are in the semantic alignment between the Akkadian transliteration and the English translation. High = perfect, clear mapping; Medium/Low = uncertain, guessed mapping, or highly fragmented sentences.
7.  **Ensure Complete Extraction:** You MUST include ALL transliteration lines from the dual-column sections. Do not leave any transliteration lines out.

**CLEANING RULES:**
1.  **Flattening:** Treat the text within your semantic chunk as a continuous stream. Use spaces to separate lines, no newlines (`\n`).
2.  **Exact OCR:** Keep typos, brackets `[...]`, half-brackets `⌈...⌉`, and special chars (š, ṣ, ṭ, ₄, ₅) exactly as seen.
3.  **Strip Markers:** Remove the original line indicators (e.g., `1`, `5`, `10`, `e.`, `r.`) from the Transliteration strings so only the Akkadian text remains.
